Stir Fry

Description

Stir frying is a Chinese cooking technique in which ingredients are fried in a small amount of very hot oil while being stirred in a wok. The technique originated in China and in recent centuries has spread into other parts of Asia and the West.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 medium onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 medium green pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 medium sweet red pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 can (8 ounces) sliced water chestnuts, drained
  • Hot cooked rice

Steps:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk the first six ingredients until blended; set aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat oil over medium-high heat; stir-fry chicken until no longer pink, 5-6 minutes. Remove from pan.
  3. Stir-fry onion and peppers until crisp-tender, 3-4 minutes. Stir cornstarch mixture and add to pan. Bring to a boil; cook and stir until thickened, 1-2 minutes. Return chicken to pan; heat through. Stir in water chestnuts; serve with rice.
